## Research detail (recon)

### Marketplace / embedded-services business models for a financial-health-aligned, curated (non-commission-conflicted) services marketplace inside a neobank for tiny businesses

**Findings**
- TAKE-RATE GAP IS THE WHOLE GAME: pure vertical SaaS monetizes at <1% take rate; marketplaces at 20%+ (Tidemark). But the take rate scales with how much of the match/transaction you own. A lead-only/discovery marketplace (NOT inside the transaction) captures only 1-3% effective take rate and needs 5x the GMV of a 10-15% transactional marketplace to make the same revenue (Tidemark/softwareplatform.net). For NetBank's curated CFO/legal/tax marketplace, the booking happens largely off-platform (high-value advisory relationships transact off-platform), so the realistic economic capture is the LEAD/REFERRAL layer, not a transactional take rate.
- THE NON-COMMISSION CONSTRAINT IS NOT JUST A PREFERENCE - FOR LAWYERS IT IS AN ETHICS RULE. Under ABA Model Rule 7.2/5.4, a lawyer may pay for leads ONLY if the fee is NOT contingent on whether the lead converts. A success/commission fee = impermissible fee-sharing with a non-lawyer; a flat or monthly fee paid regardless of outcome is permissible. This makes a FLAT per-lead or SUBSCRIPTION model the structurally compliant design - exactly the 'vetted, not commission-driven' posture NetBank wants. (americanbar.org Rule 7.2 comment; isba.org Avvo analysis)
- CURATION CREATES A SECOND LEGAL TRAP for regulated pros: objective, search-based matching is defensible, but if the platform presents a provider based on SUBJECTIVE criteria ('we recommend this lawyer', ranking by reviews) AND takes a fee, that converts a compliant lead-gen fee into an impermissible recommendation/fee-share. NetBank can curate the PANEL (who is allowed in) objectively/by vetting, but must be careful about per-query subjective 'we recommend X' framing if money changes hands. (isba.org)
- THE FEE-ONLY / CONFLICT-FREE FRAME IS A PROVEN TRUST WEDGE: in financial advice, the fee-only (no-commission) model is the canonical conflict-minimizing structure because the provider is paid for advice, not for selling a product (kitces.com, passivecapital.com). Kitces nuance worth adopting: NO model is conflict-free; the goal is MANAGEABLE conflicts. NetBank's honest positioning = 'we charge providers a flat panel/subscription fee and vet on quality, so our incentive is your financial health, not steering you to whoever pays most.'
- INCUMBENT PLAYBOOK (Xero advisor directory + QuickBooks Find-a-ProAdvisor) confirms the dominant model: the directory itself is a FREE lead-gen channel for advisors, NOT a paid take-rate marketplace. Neither vets advisors for service quality (Xero explicitly disclaims). They monetize INDIRECTLY - the advisor channel drives core SUBSCRIPTION + payments growth, and they share economics back (QuickBooks: 30% rev-share on subscriptions for 12 months, 20% of payments net revenue for 3 years). The marketplace is a customer-acquisition and stickiness engine, not a standalone P&L line. NetBank's curation/vetting is a DIFFERENTIATOR vs. these unvetted incumbents.
- THE DATA FLYWHEEL IS THE REAL DEFENSIBLE MOAT, NOT THE TAKE RATE: cash-flow data is a LEADING (predictive) risk signal - a drop in income or spend ramp shows up weeks BEFORE a missed payment appears on a credit bureau (lendflow, judi.ai). FinRegLab proved cash-flow variables added to credit scores predict SMB default more accurately, with the biggest gains for thin-file/early-stage businesses (finreglab.org). Booking a CFO/lawyer is a behavioral intent signal that layers on top of this. The winning embedded lenders (Shopify Capital $4.2B originated 2025; Parafin $25B+ in offers across Amazon/DoorDash; Wayflyer $5B) win because of DATA-ACCESS DEPTH, not capital - 'the integration layer is the real moat.' (apideck, thefr.com)
- INTENT-SIGNAL THESIS IS UNDER-DOCUMENTED PUBLICLY (a whitespace/edge for NetBank): searches found NO public sources treating 'hired a CFO/lawyer/accountant' as an explicit lending or risk trigger. The closest documented analog is accounting-data signals (payroll cycles, AR/AP aging, vendor terms, hiring of finance staff visible via QuickBooks/Xero integrations) used as maturity/momentum signals (apideck). This means the 'when you book a fractional CFO, that sharpens our model' thesis is genuinely novel - a proprietary signal competitors aren't mining, which strengthens NetBank's closed-loop moat argument.

**Patterns**
- MODEL FIT FOR NETBANK = FLAT-FEE PANEL + DATA FLYWHEEL, NOT % TAKE RATE. Because (a) advisory transacts off-platform so a transactional take rate is uncapturable/low (1-3%), and (b) commission is ethically barred for lawyers and trust-corrosive for a 'financial-health' brand, the right structure is: providers pay a FLAT subscription or flat per-qualified-lead fee to be on a VETTED panel, paid regardless of conversion. This is simultaneously the compliant model AND the conflict-aligned model AND the one incumbents (Xero/QBO) validate. Curate the panel objectively by quality; never charge for a per-query 'we recommend X.'
- MONETIZE THE MARKETPLACE INDIRECTLY, NOT DIRECTLY (the Xero/QuickBooks lesson). Treat the services marketplace primarily as a CAC + RETENTION + DATA engine, not a standalone revenue line. The real money is the core neobank P&L it feeds: deposits/interchange, and especially EMBEDDED LENDING where the marketplace's behavioral signals improve underwriting. McKinsey's 50-70% ecosystem cross-sell uplift is the indirect-monetization prize.
- THE 'LAYER CAKE' TAKE-RATE PRINCIPLE: take rate rises with how many tasks of the match/transaction you own (search -> matching -> scheduling -> payments -> financing). NetBank should deliberately decide which layers to own. Owning PAYMENT FLOW for marketplace bookings (paying the CFO/lawyer through the app) both (a) legitimately raises capturable economics into the 2-5% band and (b) - critically - creates the closed-loop data the flywheel needs, WITHOUT touching the ethically-fraught success-commission on the advisory fee itself.
- CASH-FLOW DATA IS A LEADING INDICATOR; BEHAVIORAL BOOKINGS LAYER ON TOP. The defensible underwriting/risk thesis: cash-flow events precede credit events by weeks (cause, not effect, of stress). 'Single-source underwriting is dying' - combine banking + accounting + commerce + (NetBank's novel layer) services-booking intent. Booking a turnaround CFO or a litigation lawyer is a risk signal; booking a fractional CFO during a fundraise is a GROWTH signal. Same data point, opposite vector - the model's job is to disambiguate via the surrounding cash-flow context.
- 'MANAGEABLE CONFLICT', NOT 'CONFLICT-FREE', IS THE HONEST AND DEFENSIBLE BRAND CLAIM (Kitces). Don't claim zero conflict (false and brittle). Claim: flat fees + objective vetting + customer-financial-health objective function = conflicts are structurally minimized and disclosed. This is more credible than the unvetted incumbents who quietly run 30% rev-share while telling clients nothing (QuickBooks explicitly does not notify clients of advisor rev-share participation - a transparency gap NetBank can weaponize as a differentiator).
- PROPRIETARY-SIGNAL WHITESPACE: nobody publicly models 'hired a CFO/lawyer' as a financial signal. NetBank's closed-loop (booking happens inside the bank that also holds the cash flow) is a genuinely novel, non-replicable dataset - the integration-depth moat that beats capital. Protect it: the moat is the JOINT distribution of (cash-flow trajectory x services-booking intent x outcome), which only an entity that is BOTH the bank AND the marketplace can observe.

**Numbers**
- Take-rate spread: vertical SaaS <1% vs. marketplaces 20%+ (Tidemark, tidemarkcap.com). Broad marketplace range 5-30% of GMV depending on value-add (softwareplatform.net, gurustartups.com).
- Lead-only / discovery marketplace effective take rate: ~1-3% (often <1%); needs ~5x the GMV of a 10-15% transactional marketplace to match revenue (Tidemark).
- Referral/commission norms for partner-sourced B2B SaaS revenue: 15-30% of first-year contract value for referral partners; 20-40% margin for resellers (rework.com). Subscription-services referral norm: 10-20% of first-year contract value, commonly capped at 12 months; standardized clauses ~15% of net subscription fee (referralrock, lawinsider).
- Professional-services referral fees: flat $50 (small-biz) to $500+ (enterprise) per engagement, OR 10-20% of first project; consulting finder's fees 1-5% (large/low-touch) up to ~35% (small/high-touch); agency norm most common = 10% of revenue (consultingsuccess, sakasandcompany).
- QuickBooks ProAdvisor monetization: 30% revenue share on client's QBO/QB Time subscription for first 12 months; payroll = 30% base + 15%/employee; QuickBooks Payments = 20% of net revenue for 3 years (quickbooks.intuit.com). Directory access gated to certified Gold-tier+ ProAdvisors.
- Xero advisor directory: free listing, quality-gated (staff Xero-certified + 8+ Xero clients) but service explicitly NOT vetted/guaranteed; 250,000+ accounting/bookkeeping partners; Xero cites firms offering advisory grow ~17% faster (xero.com).
- Ecosystem uplift: McKinsey estimates leading neobanks lift new-product sales within partner ecosystems by ~50-70% (mckinsey.com). Most of top-25 neobanks earn <$30 revenue/customer/year (Simon-Kucher, via crassula/markswebb) - the pressure driving diversification into marketplace/referral revenue.
- Interchange share kept by platform ~1-1.5%; B2B embedded-payment commissions 2-5%; managed payment-flow slice 0.5-2% (airwallex, fintechtris). Embedded fintech transactions projected >$7T by end of 2026 (up from $2.6T in 2021).
- Embedded-lending scale proving the data moat: Shopify Capital originated $4.2B in 2025 (up from $3B 2024); Parafin extended $25B+ in offers; Wayflyer deployed $5B+ to 5,000+ SMBs (thefr.com, apideck).
- CAC payback benchmarks (for valuing the marketplace as an acquisition channel): SMB <$15K ACV = 8-12 months; best-in-class <12 months; investors want <18. Referred leads close at ~24.7% vs. 31.3% website; max referral fee should be <= LTV x gross margin, target 40-60% of that (optif.ai, benchmarkit, referralrock).

### NetBank Services Marketplace — RECON: external partner-service categories Spanish/EU freelancers, agencies & <10-employee businesses need and pay for (need-sizing + price points)

**Findings**
- GESTORÍA / ACCOUNTING / TAX-IVA is the universal, recurring, mandatory spend — the anchor category. Every autónomo and SL files quarterly IVA (mod. 303) + IRPF + annual returns; almost all outsource it. This is the highest-frequency, highest-attach service and the natural data-richest entry point (it touches every transaction). Online gestorías have commoditized the low end (from 25-50 EUR/mo), so NetBank's edge is NOT price but curation + data integration (pre-filled books from transaction data).
- FRACTIONAL/OUTSOURCED CFO is the premium, high-margin, low-frequency category — and the single most powerful DATA SIGNAL for the flywheel. A company booking a fractional CFO signals a growth/financing/complexity inflection (raise prep, restructuring, scaling past founder-as-bookkeeper). This is exactly the intent moment a lender wants to detect. Few sub-10-employee firms can justify the cost, so positioning = 'CFO-as-a-service tier' or curated marketplace for the upper slice of the customer base (post-revenue agencies).
- LEGAL splits into two distinct products with different economics: (a) one-off transactional (contract/MSA drafting, 300-1,500 EUR per contract) = lead-gen/referral model; (b) ongoing retainer ('iguala', 50-200 EUR/mo autónomo, 100-900 EUR/mo company) = subscription/recurring-commission model. The iguala is stickier and a better flywheel fit.
- GDPR/RGPD COMPLIANCE (incl. external DPO) is a fear-driven, regulation-mandated spend with asymmetric downside (AEPD fines up to 20M EUR or 4% turnover). Low absolute cost (150-600 EUR/yr; DPO 50-300 EUR/mo) but high attach potential because it's a checkbox every digital business needs. Good 'curated bundle' filler; note AEPD's free 'Facilita RGPD' tool caps willingness-to-pay at the very low end.
- R&D GRANTS & I+D+i TAX DEDUCTIONS (CDTI, deducciones art. 35 LIS, Informe Motivado Vinculante) is the highest-value-per-event, success-fee category and a strong financial-health lever — it literally puts cash back on the customer's balance sheet (recover 12%-59% of dev spend; monetize 80% of unused deduction as cash). Relevant only to the tech/innovative slice, but the payoff is large enough to justify a dedicated curated partner. Aligns perfectly with the 'objective = customer financial health' framing because it is non-conflicted: the customer wins real money.
- PAYROLL / HR (gestión de nóminas) is a clean per-seat recurring add-on that scales with the customer's headcount growth — itself a growth signal. Triggered the moment a customer hires their first employee (a detectable event). Natural cross-sell off the gestoría relationship; per-employee pricing makes revenue grow with the account.
- INSURANCE (civil liability / RC) is a low-cost, high-frequency, easy-to-embed referral product (avg ~120 EUR/yr autónomo). Classic embedded-finance attach with established commission economics, but commoditized and low-signal — best treated as a referral line item, not a flagship.
- DEBT COLLECTION / RECOBRO is the category most directly tied to NetBank's core lending/cash-flow mission and the strongest closed-loop risk signal: >50% of EU B2B invoices are paid late and a customer handing over an unpaid invoice is a hard liquidity-stress signal. Success-fee model (no-win-no-fee) means zero customer downside, removing the commission-conflict concern. Pairs naturally with invoice/cash-flow data NetBank already holds.
- CURATION vs COMMISSION TENSION is real and category-specific. Insurance and one-off legal are commission-rich but low-signal/commoditized; CFO, R&D-grants and debt-collection are where 'vetted not commission-driven' actually creates trust AND the best data. Recommend tiering: high-trust/high-signal categories (CFO, tax-IVA, R&D, collections) curated and integrated; commodity categories (insurance, GDPR) as referral marketplace.
- SEGMENT-FIT MAP: solo autónomos → gestoría + RC insurance + GDPR (low-ticket, high-volume); agencies/sub-10 with employees → add payroll/HR + iguala legal + collections; revenue-positive scaling firms → fractional CFO + R&D grants (high-ticket, high-signal). The marketplace should surface different categories to different segments based on detected lifecycle stage — which is itself the flywheel.

**Patterns**
- FREQUENCY x SIGNAL-STRENGTH x MARGIN triage for prioritizing marketplace categories: anchor on high-frequency/high-data (gestoría/tax), monetize on high-margin/high-signal events (CFO, R&D, collections), fill with low-cost embedded referrals (insurance, GDPR). Frequency drives data; events drive lending intent; margin drives the P&L.
- EVENT-AS-SIGNAL model: each booking is a labeled lifecycle event. First-payroll = hiring/growth; CFO-booking = financing/scaling intent; debt-collection request = liquidity stress; R&D-grant interest = innovation spend + future receivable. These are exactly the features a cash-flow lender wants — the marketplace doubles as an intent/risk-labeling pipeline, not just revenue.
- NO-WIN-NO-FEE / SUCCESS-FEE pricing (debt collection 15-40%, R&D consultancy on recovered deduction) is the template for resolving the curation-vs-commission conflict: align partner payment with customer outcome so 'commission' and 'customer financial health' point the same direction. Prefer this structure wherever possible over flat referral kickbacks.
- TIERED CURATION: integrate-deeply (data flows both ways, e.g. tax/accounting pre-filled from transactions) for anchor categories; vet-and-list (curated panel, quality-gated) for high-trust advisory; open-referral (commission marketplace) for commodities. Depth of integration should match signal value, not just margin.
- PRICE-FLOOR awareness: free/cheap incumbents cap willingness-to-pay at the low end of several categories (online gestorías from ~25 EUR/mo; AEPD Facilita RGPD free tool). Compete on integration and curation/trust, never on undercutting price — the differentiator is the closed-loop data, not the rate card.
- LIFECYCLE-STAGED SURFACING: show different marketplace categories to solo / with-employees / scaling segments based on detected stage. The product personalizes the marketplace from the same transaction data that powers underwriting — one signal, two payoffs (better UX + better risk model).

### NetBank services-marketplace as a data flywheel: how partner bookings (CFO/lawyer/collections/grant/tax/HR) and their timing sharpen NetBank's risk, churn, revenue, concentration, and next-best-action predictions — with consent/privacy boundaries.

**Findings**
- CORE INSIGHT: a marketplace booking is a LABEL on otherwise-ambiguous transaction data. Bank transactions show WHAT happened to cash; a booking reveals WHY and WHAT THE OPERATOR BELIEVES IS ABOUT TO HAPPEN. It is operator intent that PRECEDES the transactions — a leading, hard-to-fake, in-app-exclusive signal that pure-transaction competitors cannot see.
- TIMING/VELOCITY is the meta-signal, not the booking itself. Days-since-onboarding, clustering (CFO + lawyer + collections in the same 2 weeks = an acute event = M&A or crisis), and whether the booking PRECEDES or FOLLOWS an observed cash-flow inflection all change the meaning. Same booking type carries opposite signals depending on co-signals.
- SEQUENCE/ORDERING tells the story arc. grant-consultant -> CFO -> IP-lawyer = a growth-capital narrative. collections -> debt-lawyer -> CFO = a distress/workout narrative. Order disambiguates intent better than any single booking.
- CFO booking = life-stage/maturation signal, bimodal. With healthy inflows + new large invoices -> growth (fundraise/scale/margin overhaul). With balance trending to zero + rising overdraft -> CFO-as-firefighter (distress). Improves: revenue forecast (structural change incoming), default risk (distress variant), NBA (growth loan vs. bridge).
- LAWYER booking = highest-information, highest-ambiguity; sub-type is everything. Corporate/M&A -> exit/acquisition/raise (positive but CHURN risk, account may close). Employment -> dispute/firing (HR distress). Litigation/debt -> being sued or customer dispute (cost shock + possible frozen funds). IP -> product launch (growth). Improves churn, default risk, concentration risk.
- COLLECTIONS-AGENT booking = the single sharpest distress/AR-stress signal. It means receivables exist that the business cannot recover itself -> a cash-flow gap is materializing NOW and a liquidity crunch is likely in 30-90 days. Strongly improves default risk, applies a HAIRCUT to revenue forecast (booked != collected), and flags concentration risk if collections target the dominant customer.
- GRANT/SUBSIDY-CONSULTANT booking = mostly growth/investment intent (pursuing non-dilutive capital: EU/Spain grants, R&D credits, Kit Digital). Signals a lumpy, LAGGED inflow in ~3-9 months and runway extension. Improves cash forecast (expected inflow), lowers default risk, enables NBA (bridge against awarded-but-unpaid grant). Caveat: can also mean desperate runway-hunting.
- TAX/ACCOUNTING-ADVISOR booking = baseline hygiene; TIMING carries the signal. On-cycle (pre-VAT/fiscal deadline) = low-information seasonality. Off-cycle + urgent = possible tax-liability shock, audit, or restructuring. Repeatedly churning through many different accountants = bookkeeping chaos = data-quality and reliability red flag.
- HR/RECRUITER/PAYROLL booking = hiring intent -> growth + rising payroll (the stickiest, most dangerous fixed cost for a small co). Improves revenue forecast (future capacity) but RAISES default risk if revenue doesn't follow headcount (operating-leverage trap). Severance/contraction-advisory booking = the inverse: contraction/distress signal.
- BOOKING != COMPLETION != RECURRENCE. A booking is intent; a completed + recurring engagement (monthly fractional CFO) is a committed state-change; a CANCELLED/no-show booking is its own NEGATIVE signal (abandonment, possible wind-down). Outbound marketplace SPEND corroborates the booking signal directly in NetBank's own P&L view.
- FLYWHEEL: bookings label ambiguous data -> outcomes (did the co actually grow/default/churn after?) become training labels -> predictions improve -> better-timed offers + better risk pricing -> better financial-health outcomes -> more trust -> more marketplace usage. Closed loop; the moat is the EXCLUSIVE, LEADING, intent-level signal layered on top of transaction data.
- OBJECTIVE-ALIGNED USE MUST BE ASYMMETRIC: because the goal is customer financial health and the marketplace is 'vetted, not commission-driven,' booking signals should trigger HELP (proactive financing, human outreach, advisory) far more readily than PUNISHMENT (credit cuts). Using 'booked a lawyer' to cut a line without other cause is both a privacy breach and a self-fulfilling distress spiral — the opposite of the objective.

**Patterns**
- BOOKING-AS-LABEL: treat every in-app partner booking as a weak supervised label on the transaction stream, and the post-booking outcome (growth/default/churn) as the target. This converts a product surface into a continuous, self-replenishing labeled training set competitors with bank-data-only cannot reproduce.
- INTENT-BEFORE-TRANSACTION: prioritize signals that reveal operator intent ahead of the cash event (grant consult before inflow, collections before write-off, HR before payroll ramp). Leading > lagging. Rank booking types by lead-time-to-outcome.
- DISAMBIGUATE-BY-CO-SIGNAL-AND-SEQUENCE: never score a booking in isolation. Always condition on (a) concurrent cash-flow trajectory, (b) booking cluster density, (c) ordering of bookings. The triple {type, timing, sequence} is the unit of inference, not the booking alone.
- EVERY BOOKING HAS A PAIRED NEXT-BEST-ACTION: collections->invoice financing / credit line; grant->bridge against award; HR->payroll financing; growth-CFO->growth term loan; distress cluster->proactive restructure + HUMAN outreach (not an upsell). Map booking type -> offer as a lookup table so risk signals become retention/revenue moments.
- CONCENTRATION COMPOUNDING: flag when a collections action or dispute-lawyer booking targets the SAME dominant customer that is the firm's revenue concentration — the entity that is simultaneously your top revenue source AND your top credit risk. This is the highest-severity composite signal.
- METADATA-NOT-CONTENT FIREWALL (privacy invariant): use only the FACT, TYPE, and TIMING of a booking as a feature. Never ingest the privileged content/work-product of the engagement (what the lawyer/CFO advised). Signal = 'booked employment lawyer'; never 'sued by employee X for Y'. Partner work-product requires separate explicit mutual consent and is never used to the customer's detriment.
- GDPR PURPOSE-LIMITATION GATE: data collected to DELIVER the booking is a distinct purpose from CREDIT-RISK SCORING. Re-purposing needs an explicit lawful basis + transparent disclosure + ideally opt-in. Profiling with legal/significant effect (credit decisions) triggers Art. 22 -> human-in-the-loop, explanation, right to contest. Don't bury it.
- SENSITIVE-CATEGORY CAUTION: legal/HR bookings imply disputes/distress that are GDPR special-category-adjacent and fair-lending-sensitive. Govern them so the marketplace never becomes an adverse-action surveillance funnel; require a non-booking corroborating cause before any negative credit action.
- ASYMMETRIC-ACTION GOVERNANCE: codify that booking signals are weighted strongly toward proactive help and only weakly (and only with corroboration) toward restriction. This simultaneously aligns incentives, satisfies the 'not commission-conflicted' mandate, reduces self-fulfilling-distress risk, and improves optics/regulatory posture.

**Numbers**

### Competitive landscape for embedded business-services marketplaces in neobanking, and the gap for a curated, AI-routed, financial-health-aligned services marketplace (CFO/legal/tax/HR) for the underserved freelancer/agency/<10-employee tier (Spain/EU first)

**Findings**
- INCUMBENTS BUILD SOFTWARE MARKETPLACES, NOT HUMAN-SERVICE MARKETPLACES. Qonto, Revolut Business, Holvi all run 'Integrations & Partnerships' hubs, but the dominant content is SOFTWARE CONNECTORS (accounting sync, HR tools, e-commerce plugins, Zapier/Slack), not booking a human CFO/lawyer/tax advisor. Revolut explicitly frames its hub as 'like an App Store' for tools. This is the core gap: nobody runs a curated HUMAN expert marketplace booked from inside the banking app for the sub-10-employee tier.
- QONTO IS THE CLOSEST EU ANALOG BUT IT'S OFFER-LED, NOT OUTCOME-LED. Qonto's marketplace mixes integrations + 'value-added financial services' = exclusive PARTNER OFFERS (insurance, payment terminals, free trial months with an online accounting firm 'Dougs'). It's a discount/offers channel curated on 'customer experience, transparent offering, quality service' criteria — but it is NOT positioned around the customer's financial-health outcome, and there's no AI routing or signal-driven recommendation. It's a static offers wall.
- US LEADERS (BREX/MERCURY) DO PERKS + LENDING-VIA-PARTNERS, NOT EXPERT SERVICES. Brex = startup PERKS marketplace (AWS/Google Ads/Slack credits, partner discounts) plus bundled in-house embedded services (expense, travel, bill pay). Mercury = minimal perks, but explicitly routes CREDIT and VENTURE FUNDING through partner lenders/VCs (Mercury Raise). Validates the 'route an external service from inside the bank' model — but for capital and software, NOT for fractional-CFO/legal/tax/HR human advice.
- SPANISH INCUMBENTS SPLIT INTO TWO UNBUNDLED MODELS, NEITHER BANK-NATIVE. Declarando = done-for-you online gestoria/asesoria (tax advisory for autonomos, ~3,300+ Trustpilot reviews, verticalized into influencers/e-commerce/creators). Holded = ERP/invoicing software that runs an ASESORIA PARTNER NETWORK (e.g. iAutonomo) connecting firms to its software. So in Spain the 'human expert + software' bundle exists — but it sits in accounting-software land, NOT inside a business bank account, and it's single-service (tax), not a multi-discipline curated marketplace.
- HOLVI IS THE ONE NEOBANK THAT SELLS A REAL HUMAN SERVICE — BUT NARROW AND SINGLE-MARKET. Holvi Zen (Finland only) = done-for-you monthly bookkeeping delivered by partner Lemon Tree Oy, bundled with account+card+invoicing. Proves a neobank CAN embed a managed human financial service. But it's sole-traders only, single-entry only, no limited companies, no employees, and Finland-only — i.e. a proof-of-concept, not a scaled multi-discipline marketplace. Holvi is now SumUp-owned, targeting the same freelancer/self-employed tier as NetBank.
- FRACTIONAL-CFO/EXPERT MARKETPLACES ARE A MATURE STANDALONE INDUSTRY — BUT FULLY DISCONNECTED FROM BANKING. Paro (~5,000+ vetted pros, <2% acceptance, ~$100M+ ARR), Toptal (top 3%, up to 40% commission), Shiny, KORE1 (placement <=14 days) all run AI-matched vetted-expert marketplaces. Crucially: web research found NO fractional-CFO marketplace that integrates banking/lending data. They match on a questionnaire, blind to the client's real cash flow. NetBank's structural advantage = routing on actual transaction/runway signals, not a self-reported brief.
- COMMISSION-CONFLICT IS A RECOGNIZED, DIFFERENTIATING POSITION. A visible sub-segment of expert marketplaces (US Fractional CFO Alliance, FractionalLeader) competes explicitly on 'zero platform fees / no commission / no revenue share — you keep 100%', signaling that take-rate conflict is a known pain advisors and buyers distrust. This directly validates NetBank's 'vetted, not commission-driven' positioning as a real wedge, AND warns that a pure commission model is the thing the market is reacting against — so monetize via subscription/lead-gen/flat-fee, not a CFO-harming take rate.
- THE COMPOUND GAP / WEDGE: every incumbent has ONE of the three pieces, none has all three. Qonto/Revolut have the bank surface + a curated-offers muscle (but software/offers, not vetted humans, no AI routing, offer-led not health-led). Paro/Toptal have the vetted-human + AI-matching muscle (but no banking data, no financial-health objective). Holded/Declarando have the human-expert + Spanish-SMB fit (but in accounting software, single-service). NetBank's defensible position = the ONLY one combining (a) bank-native booking surface, (b) curated VETTED HUMAN experts across CFO/legal/tax/compliance/HR, (c) AI routing on REAL cash-flow/runway signals toward a financial-health objective — which is also the data flywheel none of them can close because they lack one of the three pieces.

**Patterns**
- INVERT THE INTEGRATIONS HUB: incumbents' hubs are software-connector walls gated by plan tier — NetBank's wedge is to keep that surface/tiering but fill it with vetted HUMANS + AI routing, the one variant nobody ships.
- PUBLISH THE VETTING BAR as a marketed trust artifact (named criteria + acceptance rate), mirroring Qonto/Paro/Toptal — operationalizes 'vetted, not commission-driven'.
- ROUTE-DON'T-OWN: deliver CFO/legal/tax/HR via vetted external partners (Mercury/Holvi/Holded pattern) so NetBank owns routing+trust+data without building a services firm.
- MONETIZE ANTI-COMMISSION: use subscription/lead-gen/flat-fee (the high-trust-segment model) so routing incentives stay aligned with customer financial health, not take rate.
- MOAT = THE JOIN: book-a-CFO/lawyer + WHEN is a runway/growth/distress signal; it both sharpens matching and feeds credit/risk underwriting — structurally unavailable to marketplaces (no finances) or banks (route to software). This is the closed loop.
- SEQUENCE SINGLE-SERVICE-FIRST: lead with tax/accounting (Spain autonomo + VeriFactu pain, proven by Declarando/Holded/Holvi Zen), earn trust+signal, then expand to CFO/legal/HR — don't launch all six disciplines at once.
