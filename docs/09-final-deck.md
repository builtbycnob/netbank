## Slide 4 — The ontology that makes the AI possible

- **Seven objects, one spine** (the docs/05 ontology, with **Payer** elevated per docs/06): **Customer · Account/Transaction · Invoice/Receivable · Payer · Goal · Product/Loan · Decision/Agent.** A salary-bank's schema has **no object for an Invoice or a per-Payer payment-lag distribution** — so to build our first model it would first have to build that object. It **won't, not can't**: a borrow-less, health-first objective dilutes its interchange/engagement margin.
- **Palantir-style three layers, not one table** (docs/08): **Objects** (above) · **Logic** (the M1/M2/M3 models + the autonomy-ladder & Annex III §5(b)/Art. 22 rules) · **Actions** (the nine agents of docs/08). The object boundaries **enforce the leakage planes and the legal regimes** — a separation of objects carries a separation of *who's on the hook* and *what may not be a feature*.
- **The per-Payer reliability ledger is the grouping key — and the closed loop is the moat, not the ledger.** Logged append-only on the Decision/Agent object; every realized payment retrains it (docs/05); the holdout **groups by the GLOBAL Payer** (a payer in train may not appear in test anywhere in the base). **The data is commodity** (Verifactu/SII) — the moat is the loop + the objective, not the bureau.
- **Entity resolution is the unlock** (docs/06): one canonical Payer per **NIF/CIF + IBAN** turns single-customer history into a network — a private **positive+negative** payment bureau, where RAI/ASNEF are **negative-only**. AR-NIF is deterministic (build first); PSD2-remitter fuzzy match is the long pole, **unproven on our data** `[to validate]`.
- **Point-in-time discipline lives in the ontology:** every reliability feature is an **`_asof_issuance` trailing snapshot** (`timestamp < issue_date`); the realized `paid_date` is the **label, never a feature**.

**SPEAKER NOTES:** This is the Structure slide, written to kill *"an ontology is just a schema with a fancy name."* Here the object boundaries do regulatory and statistical work a flat table cannot. Palantir-style we keep three layers — Objects, the Logic on them (the models + the autonomy-ladder + the Annex III/Art. 22 rules), and the Actions (the nine agents) — and the boundaries between objects are simultaneously the **leakage planes** and the **legal regimes**: fraud is quarantined on a different object from credit (fair-lending), the network signal is `_asof`-separated on the Payer object (leakage), and the grant is human-gated on the Decision object. The most load-bearing object is the **Payer**: resolving one canonical Payer per NIF/CIF+IBAN turns one customer's invoice history into a cross-base network — a positive-and-negative private bureau where Spain's RAI/ASNEF are negative-only — and is *also* our grouping key, which stops the model memorizing "Studio Vermell pays at twelve days" and collapsing on cold-start clients. The bureau data is commodity under Verifactu/SII; the moat is the loop, and the ontology is where it's wired. Honest caveat: the deterministic AR-NIF join is real today; the fuzzy PSD2 resolution that widens the network is unproven on our data — tagged to-validate.

**DEFENSE — Q: "An ontology is just a database schema with a fancy name — why does it matter here?"**
A: "Because the object boundaries do work a flat table can't. Three are load-bearing: the boundary around fraud signals keeps them out of the credit model (fair-lending); the `_asof_issuance` rule on the Payer object keeps tomorrow's payments out of today's features (leakage — our naive-vs-grouped AUC gap is the proof we removed it); and the boundary on the Decision object is where the human-on-the-loop grant lives (Annex III §5(b) + Art. 22 — the core only *sizes and warns*). A flat schema dissolves those three. And one object — the Payer, resolved to a canonical NIF/CIF+IBAN — turns one customer's history into a network and is *also* the key we group the holdout by. The ontology is where leakage discipline and legal regimes are enforced — and the moat is the closed loop running on it, not the data."

## Slide 5 — The prototype: a clickable app and a model built to invite the leakage attack

- **Honest scope:** synthetic data proves the **method and the signal structure**, not the monetization. **WTP is our #1 weakness** (docs/01 OQ1); real-data validation is the next step, not a claim today.

## Slide 6 — Role of AI: the agentic loan journey  *(lift — full slide & doc: [`docs/08`](08-agentic-loan-journey.md); see also [`docs/04`](04-slides-mobile-experience.md) Slides 5/9)*

- **Nine agents**, application → repayment, each a contract: *signal in → bounded action → handoff → escalation.* One human gate (the **grant**, Annex III §5(b) + Art. 22) + one separate **AML** gate (Ley 10/2010 / EU AMLD).
- **Autonomy is earned and capped** — every credit-adjacent action starts at *Suggest*; the grant **can never climb past human-on-the-loop**. Modes always shown: **Predict / Recommend / Act** (docs/04 S9).
- **Collections disappears:** the same signals that priced the loan keep running, so a souring receivable is **pre-empted as a borrower-confirmed reschedule/restructure (care)** — defaults persist, the *dunning phase* doesn't. The agent boundaries **enforce** the compliance boundaries (fraud quarantined, network `_asof`-separated, grant human-gated).

**DEFENSE — Q: "Strip the word 'agent' — isn't this just a loan pipeline with a human in the middle?"**

## Slide 8 — Why it's hard to copy: the closed loop, not the data

- **The moat is a closed loop, not a dataset.** Every realized payment date retrains the per-client reliability model that prices the next bridge — we see the repayment *and* its cash-flow context, then re-aggregate the trailing point-in-time features and retrain (docs/05). The data itself is **commodity**: Verifactu/SII make invoice data visible to everyone in Spain — so *"only we see invoices"* is **FALSE**, and we say so.
- **Incumbents won't, not can't — incentive lock-in.** A monthly-salary risk model has **no object** for an invoice or a per-client payment-lag distribution (docs/05 Q5); Qonto/Revolut/Mercury monetize interchange and engagement, so a borrow-less objective is **margin-dilutive**. They could ship a runway widget next week; they won't run a P&L that profits when you borrow *less*.
- **Two moat tiers, honestly tiered.** The **closed-loop DATA moat** is the **durable** one. The **financial-health OBJECTIVE moat** is **softer** — a well-capitalised entrant could run health as a loss-leader and monetize on the card tier. We don't overclaim the second.
