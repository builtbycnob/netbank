## Slide 2 — Netflix logic, applied to banking

- **The namesake, made literal** (this is the prof's Slide-20 table, NetBank-ised — docs/13 §2.A). Netflix doesn't make you *browse a catalog* — it *recommends the next thing*. NetBank doesn't make you *fill in a loan application* — it **recommends the next financial decision**. That single swap — **recommendation, not application** — is the whole UX thesis (docs/02: decisions, not screens).
- **Movies become financial ACTIONS, not products.** The unit isn't a loan in a catalog; it's a concrete move: **bridge *this* named, SII-confirmed invoice · sweep to the tax pot · re-date a payment · or "don't borrow"** (docs/05). A bank shelves products; we surface the *action* that fits this week's cash.
- **The viewer profile becomes a per-customer AND per-Payer reliability profile.** Netflix ranks on what *you* watch; we rank on how *your clients pay* — each Payer's realized lateness *(illustrative: "Client ACME pays ~18d late on a €9k invoice", docs/05)*. A salary-bank's schema has **no object** for that profile (docs/05 Q5).
- **The ranking is calibrated risk/affordability — it SIZES & WARNS; the human GRANTS.** Like Netflix's ranker the score is only a *recommendation*; unlike Netflix, where money meets a person the non-credit core **never auto-grants — the grant is human-on-the-loop** (Annex III §5(b) + GDPR Art. 22, docs/05). Calibration is the centerpiece because a calibrated probability is what *prices the bridge*.
- **Continuous learning IS the closed-loop moat — the row the deck rests on.** Netflix re-learns from every play; **we retrain from every realized payment date** (docs/05; the same loop that makes collections' dunning phase disappear, docs/08). The divergence that matters: Netflix optimizes *more watching*; we optimize *financial health*, so we sometimes **recommend LESS** — the honest "don't-borrow" verdict. *Said out loud: the invoice data is commodity (Verifactu/SII) — "only we see invoices" is FALSE; the moat is the loop (durable) + the objective (softer), not the data.*

**DEFENSE — Q: "Isn't the Netflix analogy just a gimmick to fit the assignment title?"**
A: "No — two rows are literally our architecture, so the analogy is load-bearing. First, 'recommendation, not application' is the whole UX thesis: Netflix recommends the next thing instead of making you browse a catalog; we recommend the next financial *decision* instead of making you fill in a loan form — that's docs/02's decisions-not-screens, not a metaphor. **DEFENSE — Q: "If it's Netflix, why would your recommender ever tell me to do nothing? That's the opposite of recommending more."**
A: "Because the objective function is different — and that *is* the moat's softer tier. Netflix is rewarded by engagement, so it never says 'stop watching.' We're rewarded by financial health on an inverted flywheel (docs/02, docs/05): a good month means you borrow less and open the app less. So when the per-Payer model says the cash is genuinely coming, the next-best decision is 'don't borrow' — the tell that we're not a lender hunting volume. An interchange-or-engagement incumbent won't copy that — not because they can't ship a runway widget, but because a borrow-less P&L is margin-dilutive: won't, not can't. And I keep that tier honest — it's the *softer* moat; a well-capitalised entrant could run health as a loss-leader. The *durable* moat is the closed loop, not the objective and not the data."

## Slide 6 — The ontology that makes the AI possible

## Slide 8 — The loan journey today — rigid, opaque, one-shot (the BEFORE)

## Slide 11 — Conversational agent — gather context, not just data

- **Role: the first contact, and the only agent in the roster that talks.** It is the conversational surface over the docs/08 loop — it sits *in front of* #1 Intake / #6 Offer — and its job is to **gather context, not just data**: *why* a payment is late, *which* account a bridge should land in, *what* the owner is actually trying to decide. No form to fill — the agent extracts the structured `Invoice/Receivable` + `Payer` objects (docs/09 S4) from a photo plus a reply. ## Slide 12 — Sentiment / friction agent — it decides HOW to proceed, never whether

- **Role — detect interaction state + abandonment risk, change the pace (not the verdict).** A cross-cutting agent that reads how the *session* is going and adjusts the journey's tempo and tone. It is the operational home of two HMWs the instructor templates (docs/13 §2.E, prof Slide 24): **"reduce financial anxiety"** and the docs/02 HMW#4 *"turn a late-paying client into a solved problem, not a panic"* — because the moment a bridge request appears, our customer (a **VAT-registered micro-firm chasing a late B2B invoice**, docs/11 — *not* a VC-funded startup) is usually already stressed.
- **Signals — behavioural friction, NOT inferred emotion.** Response latency, back-and-forth corrections, repeated taps / retries, partial form drop-off, time-on-explainer, abandonment-then-return, and an explicit one-tap *"this is confusing / I'm not sure"* affordance. We frame these as **observable friction**, never as a claim to read feelings. The *direction* (rising friction tends to precede abandonment) is our **working assumption**, not a cited result; every threshold and the direction itself are **`[to validate]`** on real sessions.
- **Actions — change HOW, bounded: slow down, simplify, escalate earlier.** Slow the flow (split a dense step, defer a decision), simplify (plain-language the explainer, surface the *"why only €X?"* chevron — docs/02 principle 5), or **escalate to a human earlier** than the journey otherwise would. Each is a `Recommend` / UI move on the *reversible* surface — never an `Act` on money.
- **THE CRITICAL LINE: this agent does NOT decide loans — it decides HOW to proceed.** It can *slow, simplify, or escalate*; it can **never grant, price, decline, or move funds**. It sits one layer above the **nine-agent credit journey** (docs/08) and only ever changes their *tempo and routing* — the grant stays **human-on-the-loop** (Annex III §5(b) + GDPR Art. 22). It can pull a human in **earlier**; it can never keep one **out**.
- **It serves the inverted flywheel — and it is the SOFTER moat tier, said honestly.** Its win condition is *calmer, fewer, cleaner decisions* — a session that resolves with **lower abandonment under stress**, not more time-in-app (docs/02 “the best screen is sometimes no screen”). It is NOT our durable moat (that is the closed loop — every realized payment retraining the per-Payer model). It is the **softer** tier: a health-first behaviour an interchange/engagement incumbent **won’t, not can’t** copy, because calming a user *off* the app fights its own P&L. The data here isn’t the moat either — these are session telemetry, not the Verifactu/SII invoice feed (which is commodity anyway).
