# 10 — Value-Creation Ideas (Crazy-8s) — NetBank

> **Method:** Crazy-8s (Google Design Sprint) run as a multi-agent workflow — **8 ideator agents × 8 ideas = 64**, across distinct value lenses (underwriting · health-UX · monetization/WTP · services · network/data · distribution · agentic · strategic), then scored by a **3-perspective judge panel (VC / Product / Skeptic)**, clustered to remove near-duplicates, and synthesized into a ranked shortlist. **Final 3–5 for development = a team pick** (see **Shortlist** below). Numbers in pitches inherit the docs/01/04 sourcing discipline; €-prices tagged as benchmarks/to-validate.

Synthesis of 64 ideas across 8 lenses, scored by 3 judge panels (VC, Product, Skeptic).
`meanScore` = mean of the three judge scores (1–10). Ideas flagged **⚡hi-var** have a judge spread ≥ 3 (notable disagreement — interesting).

NetBank = AI-native business neobank + embedded lender for Spanish autónomos / agencies / sub-10-employee firms. Objective function = customer **financial health** (success = borrow LESS, open the app LESS). Moat = the **closed loop** (every realized invoice-payment date retrains a per-client reliability model that prices the next loan) + a financial-health objective an interchange/engagement incumbent structurally won't adopt. Non-credit autonomous core (forecast / tax-sweep / categorize / safe-to-borrow SIZING) is outside EU AI Act Annex III; the credit GRANT is human-on-the-loop (Annex III §5(b) + GDPR Art. 22). Everything SIZES & WARNS; only a human GRANTS. #1 self-identified weakness = **willingness-to-pay (WTP)**.

## Shortlist

Ranked 5–8 strongest, defensible ideas (human picks final 3–5). Selected for: deepening the closed-loop / health-objective moat OR credibly attacking the WTP weakness, surviving the skeptic, and not being redundant.

### 1. Tax-Sweep+ — the IVA-non-event tier (`monetization-wtp-1`) · mean 9.00
**Pitch:** The first-euro paid tier (€9-15/mo) that turns quarterly IVA into a non-event — autonomous set-aside, locked sub-account, one-tap mod-303/IRPF readiness export — priced against the gestor line item autónomos already pay, not against free neobank tiers.
**Value to customer:** Kills the mod-303 panic 3.4M autónomos live with; the right euros are already aside in their own locked sub-account, the filing pack one tap from the gestor.
**Value to NetBank:** The single clearest answer to the #1 weakness — who pays (every VAT-registered autónomo), for what (panic removal), how much (€9-15/mo) — anchored to a real €25-100/mo gestor spend, not a vibe. Highest-frequency pain → highest retention.
**Leverages:** Autonomous tax set-aside to a locked sub-account (docs/04 S5/S9) + the gestor benchmark (docs/01).
**Main risk:** Free neobanks (Qonto/Revolut) bundle tax pots; the moat is execution + the locked-account UX + the gestor handoff, not the feature itself. Must prove the €9-15 converts vs. a free toggle.
**First test:** Fake-door / Wizard-of-Oz pricing test — show the Tax-Sweep+ tier at €9, €12, €15 to a cohort of beta autónomos and measure click-to-subscribe-intent + a smoke-test charge; target >15% of VAT-registered users choosing a paid tier.
**Judge consensus:** Unanimous 9/9/9 — every panel's #1 WTP answer. No disagreement.

### 2. Concurso Radar / Client Health Badge (`network-data-moat-1`, repr. of Cluster A) · mean 8.33
**Pitch:** A green/amber/red distress flag next to every named client in the AR ledger, lit daily by a public BORME/concurso + corporate-event match on that client's NIF ("ACME SL filed a capital reduction last week"). One green primitive; also packaged as the paid **Counterparty Distress Watchtower** (`strategic-moat-2`, also 8.33) for non-borrowers and gestorías.
**Value to customer:** Weeks-to-months of warning that a paying client is going bad before the invoice flips overdue — chase, tighten terms, or stop extending credit while the receivable is still collectable.
**Value to NetBank:** The cheapest moat-realizing surface — public data, counterparty-is-the-subject (green ethics), leakage-clean (filing < issue enforced), buildable today on data.boe.es. Sharpens M1 on the exact tail risk that drives bridge LGD, and the paid Watchtower packaging is clean WTP decoupled from credit.
**Leverages:** docs/06 §3 QW#1 counterparty public-distress join (B1/D1/D3) + the AR client-NIF keys + M1; surfaced as agent #3 in docs/08.
**Main risk:** It's a daily "reason to open the app" — must stay a true warning surface, not drift into an engagement feed that contradicts the calm objective.
**First test:** Backtest — run the BORME/concurso match over a held-out set of historical receivables and measure how many eventual write-offs were preceded by a public filing, and the median lead-time. Defensible if lead-time is weeks and precision is high enough to act on.
**Judge consensus:** 8/8/9 — skeptic's top pick ("highest-power-lowest-friction signal in the deck, ship first"); all three flag it as one primitive surfaced four ways — fund it once.

### 3. Bridge fee on a named receivable (`monetization-wtp-3`) · mean 8.00
**Pitch:** The core lending line done right — a transparent factoring-style fee (1.5-3%) on a specific SII-confirmed receivable, sized by the per-client reliability model. The customer sees the fee, the reason, and the exact invoice it's secured against. The positive-signal extension is the **Reliable-Payer Discount** (`network-data-moat-4`, 7.33): cheaper because your client is proven good.
**Value to customer:** Booked-but-uncollected cash turned spendable today (a freelancer waiting ~81 days paid in days), at a fee that tracks a named reliable invoice, not a blanket risky-borrower rate.
**Value to NetBank:** This IS the business — and the closed loop monetized: losses sit in the factoring 1-3% band (vs. 5.4% consumer NPL), so the fee is mostly margin, and every advance feeds a realized pay-date back into the model that prices the next one.
**Leverages:** Per-invoice/per-client reliability model (docs/05) + human-on-the-loop bridge flow (docs/04 S8).
**Main risk:** Capital/funding for the advance book and regulatory perimeter on factoring; reliability-model accuracy directly sets the loss rate — a mispriced cold-start advance bleaks margin.
**First test:** Paper-trade the underwriting on historical data — for a sample of past receivables, compute the model-priced fee, then check realized late/loss outcomes; confirm the 1.5-3% fee covers realized losses + cost-of-capital with positive net margin.
**Judge consensus:** Unanimous 8/8/8 — "the core lending P&L done right… not a speculative add-on." No disagreement.

### 4. Gestoría Co-Pilot Channel (`distribution-growth-2`, with `strategic-moat-3` reviewer angle) · mean 8.00
**Pitch:** A free white-label dashboard that pre-fills gestorías' clients' books from NetBank transaction data (riding the mandatory July-2027 Verifactu pain) + a flat per-onboarded-client referral — turning the trusted advisor into the distribution arm into its whole autónomo book. The B2B2C variant (`strategic-moat-3`, 7.67) lets the gestor also serve as the Art-22 human-on-the-loop credit reviewer.
**Value to customer:** Their existing trusted advisor recommends the bank, and the gestoría relationship gets easier because the books reconcile automatically.
**Value to NetBank:** Directly answers the open CAC question — one gestoría deal = dozens of pre-qualified autónomos at flat, predictable cost with the trust transfer done by the incumbent advisor. The same relationship can supply the compliance reviewer role. Distribution is where thin-margin neobanks die; this de-risks it.
**Leverages:** docs/07 gestoría anchor + docs/01 CAC-channel question; docs/08 #5 human-gated grant (the gestor as reviewer).
**Main risk:** Gestorías are conservative, fragmented, and may see NetBank as a threat to their own fees; the referral economics + white-label build must be worth their channel risk.
**First test:** Sign 2-3 pilot gestorías in Barcelona/Madrid and measure cost-per-onboarded-autónomo + activation rate vs. paid-acquisition CAC; target a materially lower blended CAC and >40% activation.
**Judge consensus:** 8/8/8 — "the strongest distribution play"; product + skeptic both note the overlap with `strategic-moat-3` and recommend folding the gestor-as-reviewer insight in.

### 5. Servicing Re-Date / Borrower-Confirmed Re-Date (`underwriting-edge-6` + `agentic-automation-2`) · mean 8.00
**Pitch:** "Collections disappears," made operational. After disbursement the underwriting signals keep running; when a shortfall is predicted, the loop pre-fills a same-amount re-date to the now-realistic pay-date and pushes one notification the borrower approves with a single tap — replacing the entire dunning chase.
**Value to customer:** Instead of a dunning call and a late fee: "your client ACME now looks like day 52 — shift the repayment? tap to confirm." The adversarial collections phase is gone.
**Value to NetBank:** Lower realized losses AND lower servicing cost (no collections operation on the pre-emptible segment), term-change kept human-confirmed (Art-22-clean), and every re-date is a labeled outcome that retrains reliability. It's the structural proof of the financial-health objective an interchange/engagement incumbent won't ship.
**Leverages:** docs/08 agent #8 Servicing consuming the contagion radar + all-accounts shortfall, closing the loop back to docs/05 retrain.
**Main risk:** Depends on the shortfall forecast being accurate and early enough; a wrong re-date offer (or one that masks genuine default) erodes the trust it's built on. The contagion input is base-density-dependent — the registry/public part works earlier than the cross-creditor part.
**First test:** On historical loans, simulate the re-date offer using only signals available pre-due-date and measure how many eventual late/missed payments would have been pre-empted, and the false-positive rate of offering a re-date to a loan that would have paid on time.
**Judge consensus:** 8/8/8 — "the most viscerally felt anti-stress moment in the set" (product); all three treat `underwriting-edge-6` and `agentic-automation-2` as the same idea, two framings.

### 6. Don't-Borrow Verdict (`agentic-automation-4`) · mean 7.67
**Pitch:** Before any bridge offer renders, the Capacity agent runs an honest check: if the all-accounts forecast shows the cash genuinely arrives in time, the screen says "you don't need to borrow — €X lands Aug 14" and no loan offer is generated at all.
**Value to customer:** Saves interest the owner would have paid out of habit or panic; the bank actively tells them NOT to take its product when they don't need it.
**Value to NetBank:** The single most credible, demonstrable proof that the financial-health objective is genuine and structurally uncopyable by an interchange/engagement incumbent — the soft moat made tangible in one screen. Loans that survive the verdict are genuinely needed and lower-risk, improving book quality.
**Leverages:** docs/05 Q4 honest "don't-borrow" branch + M2 all-accounts shortfall forecast; docs/08 Capacity agent #4 (sizes, never grants).
**Main risk:** A forecast that's wrong in the "you're fine" direction is worse than wrong in the cautious direction — it could leave an owner short. Calibration and a clear "but here's a safety line if it slips" fallback are essential.
**First test:** A/B a cohort where the verdict is shown vs. not, and measure (a) realized shortfalls among "you're fine" users (must be near-zero), and (b) trust/NPS + downstream conversion to genuinely-needed bridges — proving the honest stance increases rather than cannibalizes lifetime value.
**Judge consensus:** 7/8/8 — "the single most credible proof the health objective is real" (product + skeptic); VC slightly lower (it's positioning, not direct revenue) but agrees it's the soft moat.

### 7. Calm Score (`financial-health-ux-1`) · mean 7.67 · ⚡hi-var
**Pitch:** A single 0-100 financial-health number on Home — computed from runway weeks, tax-pot coverage, and client-concentration — readable in under 5 seconds and watched trending over months, replacing anxious daily balance-staring with one honest "am I okay?" gauge.
**Value to customer:** Answers "am I okay?" at a glance and gives a concrete reason NOT to open the app when it's green and steady — directly buys back attention and lowers money anxiety.
**Value to NetBank:** Operationalizes the financial-health objective into a measurable per-customer KPI; the score's inputs (runway, tax coverage, concentration) are exactly the safe-to-borrow signals, so the trend data sharpens underwriting at zero extra collection cost. Anchors the whole calm-product experience.
**Leverages:** Home "Am I okay?" concept + the runway/burn/tax-pot engine (docs/00, docs/02); the <5s time-to-answer criterion already locked.
**Main risk:** A single number is the most copyable widget in the deck (VC's concern) and risks over-simplifying a complex position — it must be honest about what it hides (e.g. show the one factor dragging it down) and not become a vanity gauge.
**First test:** Usability test with 8-10 autónomos — show the Calm Score and measure time-to-answer "are you okay?" (<5s target) and whether the score + its top drag-factor matches their own assessment of their situation; then track whether high-score users actually open the app less.
**Judge consensus:** 6/9/8 — high variance. Product calls it "the product's soul," skeptic strong (8) noting over-simplification risk; VC lowest (6) — "the most copyable widget here." Defensible as the experience anchor, not as a standalone moat.

### 8. Statute-of-Late-Payment Enforcer (`strategic-moat-8`) · mean 7.33
**Pitch:** An automated service that detects when a customer's client breaches Spain's 30/60-day legal payment terms (Ley 3/2004; CEPYME's >85-day reality) and one-taps the statutory remedy: a compliant pre-litigation monitorio claim, legal late-interest calculation, and an evidence pack assembled from the customer's own invoice + payment-date data.
**Value to customer:** Converts the autónomo's biggest structural wound — >85-day collection on 30/60-day legal terms — into recoverable money and enforced interest, without hiring a lawyer or souring the relationship until they choose to.
**Value to NetBank:** A flat-/success-fee enforcement product with obvious WTP, built on data already in the ledger; the legal-nudge outcome is a hard, dated label (did it get them paid?) that further trains the per-client reliability model and feeds litigation/distress signals.
**Leverages:** docs/06 D7 litigation/monitorio signals + docs/05 realized payment-date ledger + docs/01 verified >85-day / 30-60-day-legal-term pain.
**Main risk:** Legal-service delivery requires a regulated partner (abogado/procurador) — NetBank facilitates, doesn't practice law; the monitorio flow must be a compliant handoff, and customers may fear damaging the client relationship.
**First test:** Manual concierge pilot — for 10-20 customers with breached-term receivables, generate the interest calc + evidence pack and route the monitorio through a partner lawyer; measure recovery rate, time-to-pay, and willingness to pay a success fee.
**Judge consensus:** 7/7/8 — skeptic top-8 ("grounded in real Spanish law and the verified pain — concrete, not grand"); all three see clear WTP on a verified wound.
